The usual way a story is told is linear. Many authors have tried to implement branching narratives, like choices in video games, or series like the Witcher. 

But most of them nowadays are just fake choices that lead to the same pre-written story.

However, in a story that is NOT pre-written, narrative branching is actually a feasible way to do this.

In a world where the story adapts to the choice of the player, each decision has its own weight, that will guide the story where it should be. No playthrough is ever the same. In fact, the same choices NEVER even lead to the same story. that's LLM!

Technically, the exploratory power of LLMs can be seen as undermining robustness, but in narrative, it is actually desirable. I'm exploiting that.
