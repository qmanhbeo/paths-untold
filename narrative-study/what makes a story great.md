## So… what makes a story *great*?

Let’s strip the fluff. It’s usually these 4 things:

---

### 1. A core question that refuses to die

Not a theme. A **question**.

* *Attack on Titan*: “What is freedom, really?”
* *Interstellar*: “What are we willing to sacrifice for survival?”
* *The Stranger*: “Does life have inherent meaning?”

Everything in the story is just… different angles attacking that same question.

If your story doesn’t have this, it drifts.

---

### 2. Characters who are trapped, not free

Ironically.

Eren isn’t free. He’s *the least free character in the entire show*.

He is:

* bound by his nature
* bound by knowledge of the future
* bound by his own definition of freedom

That’s why it works.

> Great characters don’t explore options.
> They **collide with who they are**.

---

### 3. Escalation that tightens, not expands

Weak stories:

* open new possibilities constantly

Great stories:

* **remove possibilities over time**

By the end of AoT:

* there are almost no “good options” left
* only trade-offs

That’s why the ending feels heavy, not random.

---

### 4. Retrospective inevitability

The killer feature.

After finishing, you look back and think:

> “Of course it had to end like that. There was no other way.”

Even if logically there were 1000 other ways.

That *illusion of inevitability* is the highest art.
